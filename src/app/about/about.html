About GTA Radio

The radio stations are part of the world, not just background music.

A radio station in Grand Theft Auto is a fictional broadcast that accompanies the player while driving or exploring the city. Music, hosts, ads, satire and commentary all help define the tone of a game and the era it is trying to portray.

Across the series, stations range from genre-focused playlists to talk shows and absurd commercials. That mix is what makes the soundtrack feel embedded in the city instead of sitting on top of it.

Project Goal

A final degree project with a playable archive approach.

This application is designed as a themed listening experience for video game radio. The objective is to preserve the feeling of moving through those fictional stations while presenting them in a cleaner desktop interface.

Technical Angle

Angular for interface, Electron for packaging.

The project combines a web UI with desktop delivery, allowing the interface to behave like a focused media player while keeping the flexibility of Angular components and routing.

Audio Formats

Radio assets changed format across the series.

Depending on the game, GTA radio audio has been stored in WAV, MP3 or Ogg Vorbis formats. That variation is part of the broader history of how the series handled music, storage and platform constraints.

The point is not only to listen to songs, but to recover the feeling of tuning into a station that belongs to a world with its own voice.
